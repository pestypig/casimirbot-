## Admissibility Boundary

Use these sources only to:

- define exploratory collapse-model and time-crystal nodes
- define measurable comparison quantities
- add hard falsifiers and maturity boundaries

Do not use these sources to:

- promote Orch-OR into certified neuroscience
- promote stellar observables into quantum-collapse evidence
- promote periodic biological oscillations into time-crystal claims without strict criteria

## A. Gravity-Related Collapse Foundations

1. Penrose, `On Gravity's Role in Quantum State Reduction`.
   - https://doi.org/10.1007/BF02105068
   - Use for the canonical gravitational self-energy collapse timescale proposal.

2. Diósi, `A universal master equation for the gravitational violation of quantum mechanics`.
   - https://doi.org/10.1016/0375-9601(87)90681-5
   - Use for the gravity-related master-equation baseline.

3. Pikovski et al., `Universal decoherence due to gravitational time dilation`.
   - https://doi.org/10.1038/nphys3366
   - Use for a gravity-linked decoherence mechanism that is not identical to Orch-OR.

4. Donadi et al., `Underground test of gravity-related wave function collapse`.
   - https://doi.org/10.1038/s41567-020-1008-4
   - Use for direct experimental bounds.

5. Carlesso et al., `Experimental bounds on collapse models from gravitational wave detectors`.
   - https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.94.124036
   - Use for collapse-parameter exclusion and repo falsifier posture.

## B. Orch-OR Core And Direct Critique

6. Hameroff and Penrose, `Consciousness in the universe: a review of the Orch OR theory`.
   - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24070914/
   - Use only as the positive statement of the Orch-OR hypothesis.

7. Tegmark, `The importance of quantum decoherence in brain processes`.
   - https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevE.61.4194
   - Use as the main decoherence-based critique.

8. Hagan, Hameroff, and Tuszynski, `Quantum computation in brain microtubules? Decoherence and biological feasibility`.
   - https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevE.65.061901
   - Use as the best-known rebuttal to Tegmark, still exploratory.

9. Bassi et al., `Models of wave-function collapse, underlying theories, and experimental tests`.
   - https://doi.org/10.1103/RevModPhys.85.471
   - Use as the broad model-family review when collapse math needs a mature reference separate from consciousness claims.

1. a direct measurable observable
2. a direct falsifier or exclusion criterion
3. a primary positive source and at least one critical or bounding source
4. an explicit boundary preventing stellar or gravitational observables from being relabeled as consciousness evidence
5. for Bandyopadhyay-style triplet claims, explicit pairing with mainstream time-crystal criteria and no-go sources
