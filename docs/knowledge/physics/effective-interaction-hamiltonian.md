Definition: An effective interaction Hamiltonian is the reduced conservative energy function that packages pairwise or coarse-grained interactions, often with explicit external-potential terms, after microscopic degrees of freedom have been eliminated.
Key questions: Which degrees of freedom were removed, and what external fields or potentials remain explicit in the reduced model?
