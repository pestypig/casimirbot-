Definition: Kinetic theory closure is the step where a distribution-function hierarchy is truncated or approximated so that collision terms, damping, diffusion, and source terms can be expressed in a reduced transport model.
Key questions: Which hierarchy was truncated, and what closure assumptions were used for collisions or source terms?
Notes: This is the bridge from many-body Hamiltonian descriptions into practical transport coefficients.
