Definition: Equilibrium or weakly perturbed many-body statistical mechanics starts from a many-body Hamiltonian, builds partition functions and free energies, and treats gravity, tidal, or optical forcing as external potentials.
Key questions: Which observables are derived from the Hamiltonian and when is the equilibrium limit valid?
Notes: This is a knowledge-binding definition for paper congruence, not a claim of new physics.
