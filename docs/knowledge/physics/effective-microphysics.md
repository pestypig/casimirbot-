Definition: Effective microphysics is the reduction from particle-level interactions to usable reduced parameters such as potentials, collision rates, opacity, conductivity, friction, elasticity, and strength coefficients.
Key questions: Which microscopic interactions were integrated out, and which reduced coefficients are being kept explicit?
