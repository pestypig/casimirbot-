Definition: Earth axial precession is the long-period conical motion of Earth's rotation axis in space, driven mainly by the gravity of the Moon and Sun acting on Earth's equatorial bulge.
Key questions: Is the source discussing spin-axis precession, not orbital perihelion precession?
Notes: This definition is about Earth-orientation dynamics. It should not be conflated with Mercury-style orbital precession or with ocean tides alone.
