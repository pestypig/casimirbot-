Definition: Self-gravity-shape response is the continuum competition between self-gravity, internal pressure, material strength, rotation, and external tidal forcing that determines whether a body remains irregular, flattens, or approaches hydrostatic equilibrium.
Key questions: Is the source discussing self-gravity versus strength, hydrostatic flattening, rotational or tidal bulges, or the potato-radius threshold?
