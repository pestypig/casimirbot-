Definition: The dynamic tide is the real oceanic response to lunar and solar forcing, including rotation, basin geometry, resonance, inertia, and dissipation, rather than the hydrostatic equipotential limit.
Key questions: Is the source treating the tide as an equilibrium surface or as a propagating and dissipative fluid system?
