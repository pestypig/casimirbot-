Definition: Porous rubble-pile rheology is the effective aggregate law that combines porosity, contact friction, cohesion, and rearrangement into a bulk response model for small self-gravitating bodies.
Key questions: Is the source describing aggregate rheology, porosity, friction, or contact-based deformation in a rubble pile?
Notes: This is a diagnostic effective law, not a pure fluid model and not a rigid-body closure.
