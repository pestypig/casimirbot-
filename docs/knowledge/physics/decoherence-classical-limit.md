Definition: Decoherence is the environment-induced loss of phase coherence between macroscopically distinct alternatives, which selects stable pointer states and explains why bulk observables are usually described by classical effective variables.
Key questions: Which degrees of freedom decohere, and what classical observables survive the coarse-grained description?
Notes: This definition anchors the quantum-to-classical limit used by the source text.
Validated against:
- Zurek, W. H. (2003), "Decoherence, einselection, and the quantum origins of the classical," Rev. Mod. Phys. 75, 715. https://doi.org/10.1103/RevModPhys.75.715
