Definition: The lunisolar tide-generating potential is the differential forcing from the Moon and Sun that remains after subtracting the orbital centripetal balance at Earth's center; it is the forcing field that drives equilibrium and dynamic terrestrial tides.
Key questions: Is the source discussing the forcing potential itself, the hydrostatic equilibrium-tide response, or the full dynamic tide?
