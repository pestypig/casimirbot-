Definition: Stellar chemical inheritance is the diagnostic root claim that stellar nucleosynthesis and stellar-remnant dispersal set the elemental and isotopic possibility space inherited by later molecular clouds, protoplanetary disks, planets, and prebiotic chemistry.

Primary source anchor: Burbidge, Burbidge, Fowler, and Hoyle, "Synthesis of the Elements in Stars," Reviews of Modern Physics 29, 547-650 (1957), DOI: 10.1103/RevModPhys.29.547.

First-principles bounds:

- Conservation: nuclear reaction paths must conserve charge, baryon number, lepton number, and energy.
- Reaction network: isotope abundances evolve by coupled production and destruction terms, `dY_i/dt = production_i - destruction_i`.
- Environment: a channel is admissible only where stellar temperature, density, cross sections, decay rates, and neutron/proton fluxes make it competitive with the stellar phase lifetime.
- Timescale: a reaction path can dominate only when `tau_reaction <= tau_environment`.
- Capture regime: s-process and r-process branches are separated by neutron-capture timescale relative to beta-decay timescale.
- Yield integration: later systems inherit abundance vectors from stellar yields integrated over stellar mass, metallicity, event rates, and interstellar mixing.
- Biological relevance filter: H, C, N, O, P, S, Mg, Si, Fe, Ni, Ca, Na, K, Cl, and trace metals are the main inheritance lane for later life-chemistry constraints.

Allowed claim: stellar nucleosynthesis supplies the elemental phase space within which astrochemistry, planetary chemistry, biochemistry, evolution, and consciousness can later emerge.

Blocked claims:

- stellar nucleosynthesis creates life
- fullerenes or organics in space are evidence of life
- consciousness follows directly from stellar chemistry
- Earth biology was predetermined by stellar-remnant enrichment

Graph order:

stellar nucleosynthesis -> stellar-remnant dispersal -> interstellar enrichment -> astrochemical inventory -> planetary boundary conditions -> prebiotic self-organization -> biological common ancestry -> evolved nervous systems -> consciousness hypotheses
