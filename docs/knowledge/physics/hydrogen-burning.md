Definition: Hydrogen burning is the stellar stage in which hydrogen nuclei fuse into helium and provide the main energy source for a star's early and middle life.
Key questions: Is the source discussing the main-sequence hydrogen-fusion stage or a later shell-burning phase?
Notes: This is the first major element-creating stage in the stellar branch.
