Definition: The tidal quality factor is the cycle-by-cycle dissipation summary for a body under periodic tidal forcing. Lower Q means more energy is lost per cycle.
Key questions: Is the source using Q as a dissipation summary, rather than a universal shape law?
Notes: In rubble-pile bodies, Q packages granular, frictional, and viscoelastic loss. It is a diagnostic closure, not a direct contact-dynamics solver.
Validated against:
