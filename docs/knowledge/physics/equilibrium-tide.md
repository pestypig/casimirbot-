Definition: The equilibrium tide is the hydrostatic limit in which fluid surfaces conform to equipotentials of the combined gravity and tidal potential, without the full rotating shallow-water dynamics of real ocean tides.
Key questions: Is the source discussing the hydrostatic reference state or the full forced-dissipative tide problem?
Notes: This definition keeps the equilibrium-tide limit separate from dynamic tide modeling.
