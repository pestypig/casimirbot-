# Needle Hull / NHM2 / Warp Solve Weekly Report | 2026-05-17

## Opening Bell
This week in the Needle Hull / NHM2 / Warp Solve lane was quiet in the best and most revealing sense. The repo did not rush out a fresh solve batch, a new promotion claim, or a dramatic visual reframing. Instead, the visible movement was one careful whitepaper update that tightened boundaries around what belongs to NHM2 and what does not. That may sound small, but this lane lives or dies by disciplined separation. When a speculative geometry stack keeps its ledgers clean, silence is not emptiness. It is a sign that the project is trying to let evidence carry the weight, and let unsupported meaning stay where it belongs.

## Baseline
For a first-time reader, this lane is the repo's attempt to turn speculative warp-like geometry into a bounded NHM2 shift+lapse modeling stack with explicit stress-energy bookkeeping, observer audits, and policy gates. The baseline in `docs/nhm2-closed-loop.md` and `modules/warp/natario-warp.ts` remains conservative: NHM2 is a lapse-extended Natario-style candidate lane, not a certified transport result. The repo keeps metric evaluation, source closure, mission-time products, and visualization in separate evidentiary lanes so one surface does not impersonate another.

## What it is trying to do
This week's work is trying to keep NHM2 intellectually clean: let the whitepaper absorb adjacent quantum-spacetime experiments without letting those sidecars quietly rewrite the warp lane's claim ladder. The practical aim is to preserve a readable chain from same-chart metric evaluation to source closure, observer audit, and certificate policy, while refusing to let interesting side diagnostics masquerade as transport evidence.

## Why this matters for the larger picture
That matters because the broader project is slowly teaching itself how to model speculative geometry without collapsing into atmosphere.
